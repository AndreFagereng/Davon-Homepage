STARnet

As of now, a satellite dish mostly serves as a necessary evil because other alternatives are either too expensive to install, or simply impossible due to lack of infrastructure in your area. Regardless of whether you’re connected to a power grid, or have access to cabled internet, Davon has the solution for you. Davon's STARnet technology aims to put an end to the dish, and improve the key features that made it great. Our antenna technology is integrated into the tiles of Tesla's Solar Roof. If you're the owner of a Tesla Powerwall, STARnet uses just a fraction of the household's overall consumption.

How does satellite internet work?

Rather than cable internet or telephone lines, satellite Internet uses geostationary orbit satellites (GEO). Geostationary means that the satellites are orbiting the globe in a fixed position relative to a specific point on earth. The satellites match the earth's rotation velocity, which provides a steady signal line to a given area. Since the satellite stays put at one point in the sky, the ground antennas don't have to constantly track its position.

When the STARnet is accessed, a request is sent from the device to the router which forwards it through the Davon antennas to the geostationary satellite. The satellite forwards the request to the ISP (Internet Service Provider) hub, which beams back the necessary data through the satellite. The technology constantly improves, reducing the inherent delay of the process as much as possible.

Davon's STARnet antennas uses both the Ka-band, and the Ku-band. This allow the microprocessors within the tiles to intelligently switch between the frequency range that suits your current activity the most. The Ka-band connectivity ensures high speeds when downloading files, streaming media and so on. The Ku-band is a reliable low-latency connection that is perfect for regular web pages, VOIP-services and rapid low-data transfering, such as chatting and more, and keeping you online when you want it the most: during extreme weather. This unique multi-band approach is unique to STARnet, ensuring a next-gen experience.

Solar panel

Anatomy of the solar roof integrated with Davon antennas.

  1. Tempered glass – Extremely durable and impact resistant, all while letting satellite signals and sun energy into the deeper layers.
  2. High efficiency solar cells – Produces energy to be used live or stored within the Powerwall for later use. Able to produce decent amounts of energy, even during high temperature days.
  3. Layer of antennas – Davon antennas coming together to form an array of antennas covering the roof.
  4. Color louver film – Allows solar cells and antennas to neatly blend into the roof, while still exposing them to the sun and satellites above.
