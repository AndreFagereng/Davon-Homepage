Introducing our new satellite internet technology

The internet is becoming more and more of a necessity in every part of the world. Millions of people still lack access, and for people in remote areas, even basic electric power is not a given. Fortunately, the global community is finally starting to understand the importance of green and renewable energy sources, and both the scientific community and private companies are researching new, environmentally friendly ways to reduce our dependence on fossil fuels. One of the companies that have made strides in this area is Tesla.

The new and improved Tesla Powerwall 2 makes it possible to power one or more homes without requiring a connection to a power grid. It gathers energy from a connected solar cell panel or Solar Roof, and can store electricity for later use. Davon offers an add-on solution that seamlessly integrates with the Powerwall and the Solar Roof. Consisting of an array of antennas inside the solar cells, and a router, we can provide internet connectivity to everyone regardless of geography and without the need for expensive infrastructure.

Satellite internet have made enormous progress over the latest years, as both up- and download speeds have skyrocketed, rivalling and even besting cabled connections. The main tradeoff is high delay, but as the technology progresses, we predict this will become even less of an issue in the future. Combining satellite internet and solar power in one package, the Davon STARnet is a simple solution for getting online. No matter where or how you live.
