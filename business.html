The road ahead and what comes next

We have no interest in becoming a private company, instead opting to become either a subsidary of or get incorporated into Tesla, relying on their expertise in addition to the SpaceX program to launch the necessary satellites into orbit. As the technology as of today will be too expensive for a swift global roll-out, the implementation of the Davon STARnet will be carried out in three steps.

Step 1

Start with a small scale rollout to people with expendable income. This will bring in fresh capital and create value for the brand while making research and development easier. In turn the technology behind it becomes cheaper and more efficient.

Step 2

As the technology improves, we expand the market to middle income households. As STARnet is both a technological innovation and an environmentally conscious lifestyle product, we aim at making the brand synonymous with a clean future.

Step 3

Finally, as the technology becomes ubiquitous, STARnet will be marketed to everyone. Combined with government incentives and subsidies in poorer regions, fast internet access will become easily available to everyone.

Access for everyone

Davon's final goal is internet access for everyone in a simple, easy-to-use, and economically viable solution independent to weather conditions. We focus particularly on residents in rural or remote areas without the necessary infrastructure for cabled access. Whether you live in the desert or the tundra, if there's weather, there's STARnet!
